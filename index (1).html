About

A Neighbor Who
Knows This Place

Union Township is a small, tight-knit community where people know each other, have history with each other, and do not always have the same access to the same resources or information. A trustee who pretends those differences do not exist is not serving everyone. Just the ones who already have access.

I have lived here for thirty years. I am a wife, a mother of three, and a real estate professional who spent six years watching housing affordability climb past what working families in this community can reach. I went back to school for a Master of Public Administration because I wanted to understand how systems actually work and where change is genuinely possible.

I am running because this office does real things for real people. It determines whether a neighbor in crisis gets help or gets turned away. It stewards the cemeteries where our community's history is buried. It manages public money on behalf of every taxpayer in this township regardless of how they vote.

I am not running on what I have accomplished. I am running on what I intend to do and who I will show up for when I get there.

Platform

What I Am Here to Do

Housing and Community Access

Township assistance exists to help residents through housing instability, utility shutoffs, medical hardship, and other crises. Too many people do not know they can ask for it. I will make sure they do, and I will make sure the process is approachable for everyone who qualifies.

Environmental Responsibility

The green spaces, wetlands, and natural areas in this region belong to the people who live here. Responsible land stewardship means planning for the long term, not just the next approval cycle or the next outside investment interest.

Open and Honest Government

The trustee office manages public money on behalf of every resident regardless of how they vote. That means clear communication, records that are actually accessible, and a door that is genuinely open rather than just open in name.

Civic Knowledge and Connection

People make better decisions when they understand the systems that affect them. Part of this job is helping residents know what resources they have, what their rights are, and how local government actually works.

The Office

What the Trustee
Is Responsible For

Most residents have never had a reason to know what this office does. That is worth changing. The Township Trustee is the closest point of government to where people actually live, and the responsibilities are more concrete and more consequential than most people realize.

  • Township Assistance — Emergency financial help for residents in genuine need. Shelter, utilities, food, medical expenses, and burial costs. If you or someone you know needs help, this program exists and you have a right to ask for it.
  • Cemetery Stewardship — The trustee is responsible for maintaining township cemeteries, including abandoned and neglected burial grounds. This is an act of respect for the community's history and the people who came before us.
  • Fire Protection Oversight — Ensuring fire protection services are in place and the township is covered.
  • Township Property — Managing and maintaining township owned land and facilities on behalf of every resident.
  • Budget Administration — Stewarding public funds carefully and accounting to every taxpayer in the township regardless of politics.
  • Community Navigation — Being someone residents can actually reach when they need help understanding their options, finding resources, or figuring out which door to knock on first.
